Ad-spend vs trust — the scandal radar

Heavy acquisition spend on a thin trust/compliance base is a brittle wall. That delta is where conduct scandals and RBI action land first — you take it with grounded trust, not bigger budgets.

How it works — and why it's a crack

How: a tall amber bar (ad-spend) beside a short teal bar (trust/compliance) means a player is buying growth faster than it's earning trust. The bigger the gap, the more they must keep spending just to stand still.

Why it's a crack: acquisition is brutal — Indian FinTech CAC is ₹1,000–3,000 per customer and rose 40–60% from 2023–2025 [First Page Sage]; KYC alone adds $20–30 before a rupee of revenue, and ~73% of users abandon a FinTech app within a week [Red Branch]. In a tightening RBI regime, high-spend / thin-trust players are exactly where conduct scandals and regulatory action land first — so a grounded, cited-compliance brand converts switchers at a lower CAC and a healthier LTV:CAC (keep it above 3:1) without outspending anyone.
